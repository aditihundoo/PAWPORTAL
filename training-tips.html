Basic Training Tips

Training is essential for a happy, well-adjusted dog and a harmonious home. Here are some foundational tips to help you and your new companion succeed together!

Why Training Matters

  • Builds trust and strengthens your bond.
  • Ensures your dog's safety and well-being.
  • Prevents unwanted behaviors and stress.

Basic Commands Every Dog Should Know

  1. Sit: Hold a treat above your dog's nose and move it back over their head. As they sit, say "Sit" and reward.
  2. Stay: Ask your dog to sit, then say "Stay" with your palm out. Take a step back, reward if they don't move.
  3. Come: Use a happy voice, say "Come," and reward your dog when they come to you.
  4. Down: Move a treat from your dog's nose to the floor. As they lie down, say "Down" and reward.
  5. Leave it: Place a treat in your closed hand. When your dog stops trying to get it, say "Leave it" and reward with a different treat.

House Training

  • Take your dog outside frequently, especially after eating, drinking, or waking up.
  • Reward immediately after they go in the right spot.
  • Supervise indoors and interrupt accidents with a gentle "outside" cue.
  • Be patient‚Äîaccidents happen!

Socialization

  • Expose your dog to new people, places, and other animals in a positive way.
  • Start slow and use treats and praise.
  • Never force interactions; let your dog set the pace.

Common Challenges

  • Barking: Identify the cause. Reward quiet behavior and avoid yelling.
  • Chewing: Provide plenty of chew toys and redirect from inappropriate items.
  • Jumping: Ignore jumping, reward sitting or calm greetings.

A: No. Use positive reinforcement and redirect unwanted behavior. Patience is key.
