Health & Nutrition Guide

Keeping your dog healthy starts with good nutrition, regular exercise, and preventive care. This guide covers the essentials to help your furry friend live a long, happy life!

Why Health & Nutrition Matter

  • Proper nutrition supports growth, energy, and immune function.
  • Healthy habits prevent disease and improve quality of life.
  • Wellness routines strengthen your bond with your dog.

Key Nutritional Needs

  • Protein: Essential for muscle development and repair.
  • Fats: Provide energy and support skin and coat health.
  • Carbohydrates: Source of energy and fiber for digestion.
  • Vitamins & Minerals: Support overall health and body functions.
  • Water: Always provide fresh, clean water.

Healthy Diet Tips

  • Feed a balanced, age-appropriate commercial dog food or consult your vet for home-cooked diets.
  • Avoid table scraps, especially toxic foods (chocolate, grapes, onions, etc.).
  • Measure portions to prevent obesity.
  • Use treats in moderation and choose healthy options.
  • Transition foods gradually to avoid stomach upset.

Exercise & Wellness

  • Daily walks and playtime keep your dog fit and mentally stimulated.
  • Adjust exercise to your dog's age, breed, and health.
  • Provide toys and activities to prevent boredom.

Preventive Care

  • Schedule regular vet checkups and vaccinations.
  • Use flea, tick, and heartworm prevention as recommended.
  • Brush your dog's teeth and groom regularly.
  • Watch for changes in appetite, behavior, or energy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I feed my dog?
A: Most adult dogs do well with two meals a day. Puppies may need more frequent feedings.
Q: Can I give my dog human food?
A: Some human foods are safe, but many are toxic. Always check before sharing and avoid table scraps.
Q: How do I know if my dog is overweight?
A: You should be able to feel (but not see) your dog's ribs. Ask your vet for a body condition assessment.
